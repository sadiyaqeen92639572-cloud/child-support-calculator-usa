How Arizona Child Support Is Calculated

  1. Determine each parent's Child Support Income (gross income from all sources).
  2. Combine both parents' Adjusted Child Support Income to get Combined Adjusted Child Support Income.
  3. Look up the Basic Child Support Obligation from the official Schedule of Basic Support Obligations for that combined income and number of children.
  4. Each parent's proportionate share equals their income divided by the combined income, applied to the Combined Child Support Obligation.
  5. Apply the Parenting Time Table adjustment: the number of parenting-time days for the paying parent maps to an adjustment percentage (0% at 0-19 days up to 50% at 164+ days), multiplied by the Basic Child Support Obligation and subtracted from that parent's share.
  6. Apply the Self-Support Reserve Test: if the result would leave the paying parent below $1,685/month, the court may reduce the order.

Arizona Child Support Worksheet Walkthrough

Example: combined income $8,000/month, 2 children, Parent A earns $5,000/mo (62.5% share) and has 110 parenting-time days/year, Parent B earns $3,000/mo (37.5% share).

  1. Basic Child Support Obligation for $8,000 combined, 2 children: $1,622/month.
  2. Parent A's proportionate share: 62.5% × $1,622 = $1,014.
  3. 110 days falls in the 100-114 day bracket → 17.5% adjustment: $1,622 × 0.175 = $284.
  4. $1,014 − $284 = $730/month guideline child support paid by Parent A.

FAQ

How is child support calculated in Arizona?

Apply the Self-Support Reserve Test: if the result would leave the paying parent below $1,685/month, the court may reduce the order.

How does Arizona's Parenting Time Table work?

Arizona's Parenting Time Table maps the paying parent's annual parenting-time days directly to an adjustment percentage — 0% at 0-19 days, rising in steps up to 50% at 164 or more days per year. That percentage is multiplied by the Basic Child Support Obligation and subtracted from the paying parent's proportionate share, so more parenting time directly lowers the guideline amount.

What counts as Child Support Income in Arizona?

Child Support Income in Arizona is gross income from all sources — wages, self-employment, bonuses, and other resources — before taxes, not net take-home pay. Both parents' Child Support Income is combined to look up the Basic Child Support Obligation on the official schedule.

What happens if combined income is above $30,000/month?

The official Arizona schedule of Basic Support Obligations is built out through $30,000/month in combined Child Support Income. Above that ceiling, the schedule doesn't provide a table value, so courts use discretion to set an appropriate amount rather than an automatic formula extension.
