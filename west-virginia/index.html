How West Virginia Child Support Is Calculated

  1. Add both parents' monthly gross income (minus allowed deductions such as preexisting child support and student loan payments) to get combined adjusted gross income, then look up the West Virginia Monthly Basic Child Support Obligations table for the total number of children.
  2. Each parent's share of that amount (plus work-related child care and extraordinary medical/other expenses) is their percentage of combined adjusted gross income.
  3. If the paying parent's own adjusted gross income is below $2,600/month, an Ability to Pay Calculation applies: their spendable income (80% of their income) minus the $997 Self-Support Reserve (floored at $50) caps the final order, if that figure is lower than the standard recommended amount.
  4. Above $35,000/month combined income, an exact extended formula applies: the $35,000-level amount plus a fixed percentage of the income above $35,000 (0.088 for one child, up to 0.196 for six).
  5. This calculator models Worksheet A, used when the parent with less time has under 35% (127) of the year's overnights. Extended Shared Parenting (35%+ overnights) and Split Physical Custody use different worksheets, not modeled here.

West Virginia Child Support Worksheet Walkthrough

Example: Parent A earns $3,000/month, Parent B earns $2,000/month (combined $5,000). There are two children, who live primarily with Parent B.

  1. Combined adjusted gross monthly income: $3,000 + $2,000 = $5,000.
  2. Basic Child Support Obligations table at $5,000 combined, two children: about $1,181.
  3. Parent A's income share: $3,000 / $5,000 = 60%.
  4. Parent A pays: $1,181 x 60% = $709/month.

What is West Virginia's Ability to Pay Calculation?

When the paying parent's own adjusted gross income is below $2,600/month, West Virginia caps the support order at that parent's spendable income (80% of their income) minus a $997 Self-Support Reserve (floored at $50), if that figure is lower than the standard recommended order.

What happens above the $35,000/month cap in West Virginia?

W. Va. Code 48-13-303 provides an exact extended formula: the amount at the $35,000 combined-income level, plus a fixed percentage of income above $35,000 (ranging from 8.8% for one child to 19.6% for six children) -- not left entirely to court discretion.

What is West Virginia's Extended Shared Parenting worksheet?

When the parent with less time has 35% or more of the year's overnights, West Virginia uses Worksheet B instead: the basic obligation is multiplied by 1.6, and each parent is credited for the share of time they retain the child, with the final amounts offset. This is different from the standard Worksheet A this calculator uses.
